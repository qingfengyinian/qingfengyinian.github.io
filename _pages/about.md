# About Me

I am a second-year M.S. student at the School of Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, [USTC](https://en.ustc.edu.cn/), advised by Prof. [Tong Xu](http://staff.ustc.edu.cn/~tongxu/).

My research lies at the intersection of **learning theory and large language models**. I am interested in using theoretical analysis to understand the mechanisms behind how large models learn and behave, and in turning that understanding into more principled and effective algorithms. As I am still early in my research, I try to keep a broad view across the pipeline of how these models are trained and adapted, rather than committing to a single narrow topic.

So far, I have worked mainly on **sequential (lifelong) model editing**, where I use theoretical analysis to study the *stability* of the editing process — understanding what governs whether a model stays stable or degrades as edits accumulate, and turning these insights into more stable editing algorithms. More recently, I have been moving toward more fundamental questions about the **optimization dynamics of neural networks** — how the loss landscape and the behavior of optimization algorithms shape the training process, which becomes a particularly central concern in large-scale pre-training.

If any of this resonates with you, or if you have related problems you would like to think through together, please feel free to reach out — I am always glad to discuss and collaborate.
